Blackberry Pie
3 ½ cups fresh blackberries
2 cups frozen blackberries, let it sit in room temperature till half soft (put in blender, add water if necessary)
1 ¼ cups white sugar
4 Tbsp cornstarch
2 Tbsp blackberry brandy (if don’t have, use water or regular brandy to mix with cornstarch)
1 tsp nutmeg
1 Tbsp cinnamon
1/2 lemon, di peras
A pinch of salt


Directions:
1. Puree 2 cups of the frozen blackberries (if you have enough fresh blackberries you can use it)
in a blender until liquid. Pour into a saucepan. Whisk in 1 ¼ c sugar. Cook over a medium heat until boiling, stirring constantly and scraping bottom. 
Keep at a boil for 5 minutes, and then remove from heat.
2. Mix cornstarch and brandy + water (until liquid) in a cup. When well mixed, whisk into pan; stir until mixture returns to a boil. Remove from heat. 
Stir in the nutmeg and cinnamon. Check the consistency of the mixture; it should be like a thin pudding. If necessary, add cornstarch and water mixed at a 2:1 ratio to thicken. 
Let it cool.
3. Add the 3 ½ cups fresh blackberries to the cool mixture.
4. Fill the pie shell with the blackberries mixture.
5. Cover the edges with aluminium foil. Bake in a preheated 400 F oven for 20 minutes. Rotate the pie.
Take the aluminium off. Reduce oven to 350 F, continue baking for 30-35 minutes (until golden brown).
