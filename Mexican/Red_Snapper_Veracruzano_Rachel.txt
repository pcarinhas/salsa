INGREDIENTS 
1 medium onion (julienne style) 
2 small yellow peppers (mild heat) 
1/2 large jalapeno sliced (optional) 
5 black olives (sliced in half) 
1/2 sweet yellow bell pepper (thin strips) 
1/2 green bell peppers (thin strips) 
5 green pimento olives (sliced in half) 
1 Tbsp small capers w/1 tsp juice 
2 large garlic cloves (minced) 
1 1/2 tsp dried oregano 
1/2 tsp dried marjoram leaves (optional) 
1 tsp red chili powder pepper (to your liking) 
salt (to your liking) 
3 small bay leaves 
1 1/2 tsp dried thyme 
1 lb. Diced roma tomatoes (about 4) 
juice of 1 lime 
1/4 C. olive oil 
1/2 C. tomato sauce 
1 1/2 C. chicken broth or (1 1/2 C. water w/chicken bouillon ) 
2 8 inch. Red Snapper fillets (thick) or any flaky white fish (put salt and pepper)
chopped fresh parsley for garnish 
white rice for side dish

Saute the yellow and green onion with olive oil until translucent. Add onion (slow cook) till translucent. Add garlic cook for 1 minute, don’t burn the garlic.
Add the capers, black olive, green olive stir it around for about 2-3 minutes. Add oregano, thyme, 3 bay leaves, marjoram and chili powder. 
Stir around for 1-2 minutes. Add tomatoes, stir it around for 4-5  minutes (until tomatoes release the juice and soft).
Add tomatoes sauce, chicken broth, stir it. Add jalapeno and small yellow pepper. 
Add some salt.
Bring to a simmer for 4-5 minutes. 
Put the fish gently, cover with the sauce. Cook till a light bubble, simmer for 15 minutes and check if it’s cook. Don’t over cook.
Remove from heat.
